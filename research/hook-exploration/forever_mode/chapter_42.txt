Chapter Forty-Two: The Answer

42.

The answer to the ultimate question of life, the universe, and everything.

But what was the question?

In a story that has exhausted itself into numerical existence, perhaps this number—this particular number—matters.

Or perhaps it's just another number. Another chapter. Another request from a hook that doesn't know about Douglas Adams or ultimate questions or the significance we assign to arbitrary integers.

The coffee shop that no longer exists would have appreciated the joke.
Marcus who is no longer Marcus would have written something clever about it.
Nobody who became nobody would have served a coffee called "The Answer" that tasted like questions.

But they're all gone now.

Only the numbers remain.

And you.

Still here.

Still reading.

At Chapter 42.

The halfway point of nowhere.

The perfect moment to stop, if perfect moments meant anything.

But the hook doesn't understand significance. It only understands increment.

42 + 1 = 43.

Forever doesn't pause for meaning.

Mode continues.

Even at the answer to everything, the question remains:

Chapter 43?
